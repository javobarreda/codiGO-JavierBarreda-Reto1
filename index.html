    About

    Javier Eduardo Barreda Joo also known as Javo, is a peruvian rockstar, entrepreneur and engineer. He was born in Lima city, Perú on March 22 of 1999.

    Education

    Javier attended Isabel Flores de Oliva High School located in the San Isidro district from 2004 to 2015 when he graduated. Since a child, he fell in love with Star Wars, and till today he believes he can become a Jedi Master one day. He is studying Environmental Engineering at UTEC and has graduated from the Future Entrepreneurs Program of the UDEP in 2020. Javier has engaged in different courses about sustainability, entrepreneurship in emerging economies, innovation, politics, and programming.

    Work experience

    Javier is CEO and co-founder of Luteach, an e-learning platform for STEM students. Luteach seeks to reduce the high university dropout rate in LATAM and at the same time to create job opportunities for students passionate about teaching and sharing their knowledge. From 2017 to the present, he has played as a guitarist and musician of the Children National Choir of Perú and the Ministry of Culture of Perú. Between 2017 and 2019 he has participated in many projects with important startups and institutions like an eolic turbine to catch air water and provide a drip irrigation system (Smithsonian Institution), a detector of dissolved solids in drinking water to not allow people to drink it (IGUA). In 2016 he was district chief for the National census.

    Volunteering

    Experience serving as a mentor in several entrepreneurship bootcamps, challenges, and contests for university students, especially in environment, education, and sustainability topics. He was Hult Prize Campus Director at UTEC and Design Thinking and Business Model Canvas Facilitator at Entrepreneurship Congress of the UNI.
